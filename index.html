Frequently Asked Questions

What type of grinder delivers the best results?

We recommend starting with a quality burr grinder, specifically the Baratza Encore ($169) or 1Zpresso JX-Pro ($159) for manual grinding. These provide consistent particle size and are suitable for most brewing methods. Avoid blade grinders as they produce inconsistent results.

How do I determine the right grind size for different brewing methods?

Each brewing method requires specific grind sizes: Fine for espresso (like table salt), medium-fine for pour-over (like sea salt), medium for drip coffee (like beach sand), and coarse for French press (like kosher salt). Our comprehensive guide includes visual references and exact grinder settings for 12+ brewing methods.

How long does freshly ground coffee maintain peak flavor?

Ground coffee begins losing flavor compounds within 30 minutes. For optimal taste, use within 15 minutes of grinding. Store whole beans in an airtight container away from light and heat, and only grind what you need for each brew.

What's the minimum investment needed to start?

A quality home setup requires approximately $200-300 initial investment: $150-170 for a good burr grinder, $30-50 for a scale, and $20-80 for a brewing device. This setup typically pays for itself within 2-3 months through savings on cafe purchases.

How long does it take to master proper grinding technique?

Most users achieve consistent results within their first week using our guide. Basic proficiency takes about 3-4 brewing sessions, while mastery of advanced techniques typically develops over 2-3 weeks of regular practice.
